My Ski Life

Fresh powder, steep lines, and the mountains of Utah and Colorado

Why I Ski

A bluebird day on the mountain.

There is nothing quite like clicking into your bindings at the top of a mountain, taking a breath of crisp alpine air, and pointing your skis downhill. Skiing has been part of my life for years, and living near both Utah and Colorado makes it easy to feed the obsession.

Whether it is a powder day floating through knee-deep snow or a spring afternoon when the whole mountain feels like a party, skiing never gets old.

Top Resorts I Have Loved

Wide open bowls and tree runs.

  1. Park City Mountain, Utah
    • World's largest ski resort by acreage
    • Great variety from beginner to expert terrain
    • Easy access from Salt Lake City
  2. Snowbird, Utah
    • Known for steep and challenging runs
    • Over 500 inches of snowfall per year
    • The tram is iconic and a must-ride
  3. Vail, Colorado
    • World-class back bowls stretching for miles
    • European-style village atmosphere
    • Great for multi-day trips
  4. Breckenridge, Colorado
    • Highest chairlift in North America
    • Charming town with great après-ski
    • Tons of terrain for all ability levels
